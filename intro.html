1  Industry Project

Industry project is a form of course assessment where students work on real business problems in collaboration with industry partners (or clients) where they apply learning from the course in solving the business problem faced by the client.

Industry project constitutes a significant portion of the overall course grade. Often students work in groups of 3-5 students that will be determined by the class size and the scope of the project.

In the course, students work on industry projects throughout the semester. Project work will often begin in the second or third week and end in the last week of the semester. There will be multiple milestones throughout the project time. Each of these milestones is important as it counts towards the grade in the project.

An industry project is a holistic and authentic assessment. In this project, students apply their hard skills in real business settings. At the same time, students pick on soft skills during the project management process. While hard skills demonstrate students’ understanding of the subject matter, soft skills are essential in navigating the demanding and challenging aspects of the workplace.

1.1 Objective of Industry Project

The objective of industry project is to provide students with hands-on learning experience.

Hands-on Learning
  • Students usually work in groups.
  • Students interact with peers (e.g., group members, clients, instructors) to solve business problems.
  • Students ask questions based on observations and interactions.
  • Students collect data and provide solutions to real business problems.

An industry project is an activity-based assessment task that is authentic. In a pedagogical learning environment such as a university, students often are puzzled by how to apply the theories, concepts, methods, and skills learning in the classroom environment to solve business problems or to explain natural phenomena. Industry project that forms one significant part of the course assessments attempts to address this concern.

Learning outcomes of an industry project are three folds.

  • First, a real business problem faced by an industry partner (or client) is assigned. Students attempt to arrive at possible solutions via peer interactions. Such peer interactions involve working in groups, talking to the client and its customers, and seeking clarifications from the course instructor.

  • Second, students apply their learning from courses to explain the fit of solutions to the client’s business problem. To that end, they critically analyze the feasibility of theories, concepts, methods, and skills in optimally solving the business problem.

  • Third, through peer interactions and the application of course learning, students develop a complete understanding of the problem and make meaning of their learning experience individually. Students exhibit such understanding and meaning via course deliverables such as reports, presentations, peer evaluations, and feedback.

1.2 Who are Industry Partners

Industry partners are firms/companies/organizations/startups that form an integral part of an academia-industry relationship. Based on course structure, such an academic-industry partnership may involve public policymakers (such as government organizations). Sometimes we will refer to industry partners as simply clients.

In designing a problem for an industry project in a course, instructor starts the process way before the semester begins. Instructor gets in touch with possible clients, and explores the business problems faced by them. At the same time, instructor critically assesses the fit of the course contents with the business problems. This process is arduous and there is a lot of back and forth communication between the client and the instructor before the latter finalizes the client for industry project.

The relationship between a client and academia is that of a mutual respect and benefit. Clients are investing their valuable time, resources, and knowledge with us. Therefore, we must be respectful to them and give due diligence in solving the assigned business problem. At the same time, clients are getting valuable insights into their business operations from innovative and out of the box solutions provided by students.
