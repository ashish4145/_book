13  Academic Misconduct

In your project deliverables (see Chapter 3), we do not expect students to indulge in any kind of academic misconduct. You must adhere to academic integrity, producing and submitting your own academic work. Students whose works show dishonesty will face severe penalties.

13.1 Types of Misconduct

Following are the common types of academic misconduct that you should avoid in project deliverables.

13.1.1 Plagiarism

What is it:

  • Present someone else’s work or ideas as your own.

How to avoid:

  • If you are using information from external sources (e.g., academic articles, business sources, websites), then reference them properly.
  • Do not use unauthorized data in your work.

13.1.2 Collusion

What is it:

  • Unauthorized collaboration in preparation or presentation of work.

During mid-semester presentations, students often complain about other groups stealing their ideas. The instructor will know if you steal other groups’ ideas in your deliverables.

How to avoid:

  • Don’t steal other groups’ ideas.
  • Don’t copy other groups’ work (e.g, design, layout, examples.)
  • If same materials (e.g., data, report, statements) are used across groups, make sure your data story-telling is distinct irrespective of similar analyses.

13.1.3 Contract cheating

What is it:

  • Contract cheating is the use of outsourced material for the purpose of submission by a student for assessment.

How to avoid:

  • Don’t do it.
  • You use technologies (such as ChatGPT, Bard, or any other AI based writing tools) to supplement your learning. However, avoid submitting direct output from them as if it is your own work. Instructors have access to tools that differentiate AI generated text from human generated writing.

13.2 Penalty

Penalties for academic misconduct are severe. The immediate repercussion is a zero grade on the course project. Failing the course project may lead to failing the course as the course project will have substantial weight toward the overall course grade.

Depending on the severity of the academic misconduct, the course instructor may report it to the university. The university may expel you from the degree program you are enrolled in.

Group Penalty

In case of academic misconduct, the immediate penalty applies to the entire group, i.e., all members from that group receive zero grades on the course project.
