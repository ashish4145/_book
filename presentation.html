7  Presentation

Groups will be presenting twice during the semester. First, mid-way through the semester, all groups will update on their progress through a formal mid-semester presentation. Second, at the end of the semester, all groups will present insights and strategies from their final report.

Both these presentations are formal. They will take place in the class. Clients, instructors, and students will be present during the presentations. It is mandatory to attend these presentations.

7.1 Mid-Semester Presentation

The main objective of the mid-semester presentation is to evaluate groups’ progress in the project. Assessment is done based on each group’s understanding of the client’s business problem and a general approach taken for business solutions.

It is recommended that groups should not have more than five slides (excluding title and ending slides). Groups should use this session to show their deep understanding of the business problem and seek clarifications from the client regarding their intended solutions.

7.2 Final Project Presentation

The main objective of the final project presentation is assessment of group’s oral skills in communicating main insights of the report. This presentation will take place on the last day of the class. Instructors, clients, and students will be present. Attendance is mandatory.

It is recommended that the group should have 10-15 slides (excluding title and ending slides). During this presentation, the group should focus on their solutions and strategies. They should skip some of the topics presented during the mid-semester presentation. Also, there will be no Q&A session for the final presentation. At the end of the presentation, the client will provide a general comment (mostly positive feedback like a pat on the back.)

Groups are strongly advised to use visual elements (such as figures, tables, and videos) to make their presentation engaging.

7.3 Summary

The oral presentation is an essential skill in a teamwork. While it may take some time to synthesize written information in reports, verbal communication can get that idea across quickly.

Therefore, all the team members in a project group are strongly advised to participate in these presentations.
