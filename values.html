11  Value of Project

Learning is more effective when you get to apply directly your acquired skills. In learning by doing, you also indirectly develop some other skills. Therefore, an industry project in a course provides you an experiential learning environment where you realize its value by acquiring two kinds of skills.

  1. Applied Skills: Learning to apply your skills in a real business environment, and
  2. Soft Skills: Acquiring essential soft skills that help you in your career.

11.1 Applied Skills

The course will expose you to theories, concepts, skills, tools, and technologies in a pedagogical environment to understand the business world. Project-based learning enables you to apply these classroom skills in the real business environment. It helps you bridge the gap between theory and practice. In the process, you learn the following skills.

  1. Questioning: In industry project, you question everything. The main goal is to ensure that you are solving the right problem. You learn to define problems. You learn to raise some of the important questions that sets the foundation for solution need, its justification, and contextualization.
What is the need for solution
  • What is the basic need?
  • What is the desired outcome?
  • Who stands to benefit and why?
What is the justification
  • Is the effort aligned with the client’s business strategy?
  • What are the desired benefit to the client, and how do we measure them?
  • How do we ensure that the solution is implementable/feasible?
What is the context
  • What approaches the client has tried?
  • How the client’s competitors have approached the similar or same problem?
  • What are the constraints?
  1. Critical thinking about the problem: Once you have clear answers to the above questions, you write the problem statement, i.e., you describe the problem entirely. This process will require you to think about the business situation critically.
Defining problem critically
  • What is the root cause of the problem?
  • What are the requirements for the solution(s)?
  • Which parties we need to engage with?
  • What information do we need to approach the solution(s)?
  • How do we measure the success of proposed solution(s)?
  1. Creative problem-solving abilities: Working in a real business environment is challenging as you are faced with known knowns, known unknowns, and unknown unknowns. In such a situation, you can use creative problem-solving abilities. Based on your understanding of business problems, you explore potential solutions using divergent and convergent thinking. You generate a lot of ideas using divergent thinking. Subsequently, you evaluate options and make decisions based on convergent thinking.
Steps of creative problem-solving
  • Phase-1 (Divergent Process)
    • Clarify and identify problem
    • Research problem
    • Formulate creative challenges
    • Generate ideas using divergent thinking. You can use brainstorming for this process.
  • Phase-2 (Convergent Process)
    • Refine ideas
    • Select those that best fit to solve the business problem.

11.2 Soft Skills

Soft skills are valuable individual assets that companies implicitly seek in job candidates during the interview process. Listen to this video from a guest lecture in one of my courses where the industry speaker passes a remark on the need for soft skills casually.

Soft skills are individual traits that enable a person to perform well, especially in teamwork settings.

In classrooms, you may not be taught about these skills explicitly. However, you will acquire and develop some of these skills. In course project, which is a group-based task, you may acquire these soft skills.

  • Punctuality
    • Complete a task or fulfill an obligation on or before deadline.
    • Time management skills.
  • Emotional Well-being
    • Dealing with contrary viewpoints.
    • Ability to take criticisms positively.
  • Leadership Qualities
    • Accomplishing goal through the direction of human assistants.
    • Ability to resolve conflicts constructively in a group setting.
  • Networking
    • Meeting people and sharing information.
    • Mentality to build long-term relationship.
  • Empathy
    • Ability to understand and share the feelings and emotions of others.
    • Applying the interpersonal compassion and understanding that are fundamental to social interactions and emotional well-being.
  • Originality
    • Ability to create something new and unique.
    • Originality is distinct from creativity, which involves combining pre-existing elements in novel ways.
  • Project Management Skills
    • Managing complex project in a team.
    • Understanding the team dynamics to accomplish the task.
  • Social Skills
    • Ability to interact and communicate with others in everyday life.
    • Ability to contribute to the discussion with your own ideas and thoughts.
  • Data Literacy
    • Ability to comprehend and tell story from data.
    • Being not afraid of numbers.
  • Technology Savvy
    • Being comfortable with using new technological tools.
    • Seeing technology as a helper rather than as an annoyance.
